Gas hits a 2026 record as the Hormuz disruption grinds on

Facts

  • AAA pegged the national average near $4.26 a gallon in early June, up roughly 34% from $3.18 a year earlier; the average set a 2026 record above $4.56 in late May.
  • Brent crude jumped more than 4% to above $97 Monday after the Iran-Israel exchange, having slid to about $93 Friday on demand worries.
  • Shipping through the Strait of Hormuz — normally ~25% of seaborne oil — has been largely blocked since late February; the IEA has called it among the largest supply disruptions in oil-market history.

Left view

Mother Jones and Vox link record pump prices directly to the decision to go to war with Iran, arguing the energy shock is a self-inflicted cost now hitting working households; they push for releasing reserves and demand-side relief.

Right view

The Washington Examiner and Reason-aligned voices blame the supply squeeze on Hormuz and constrained domestic production, calling for more drilling and faster permitting rather than reserve releases; some fault the administration's deal-making for prolonging uncertainty.

Watch for

Forecasters say a durable ceasefire could pull pump prices back within weeks, while a wider conflict risks $5-plus national averages heading into the July 4 travel peak.
